# Internal Communications Skill

## Overview

This skill helps create professional internal communications following company tone, format, and distribution guidelines.

## When to Use This Skill

- Weekly/monthly status updates
- Team announcements
- Leadership updates (3P format)
- Company newsletters
- Project updates
- Milestone announcements

## Communication Types

### 1. Weekly Status Update (3P Format)

**Progress, Plans, Problems**

```markdown
# Weekly Status Update - [Team Name]
**Week of**: [Date Range]
**Author**: [Name]

---

## Progress (What we accomplished)
- Completed [feature/task] - [impact/result]
- Shipped [deliverable] - [metrics if available]
- Resolved [issue] - [outcome]

## Plans (What we're working on next)
- [Priority 1] - [Expected completion]
- [Priority 2] - [Expected completion]
- [Priority 3] - [Expected completion]

## Problems (Blockers & risks)
- **[Blocker]**: [Description] - Need: [What help needed]
- **[Risk]**: [Description] - Mitigation: [Plan]

---

**Key Metrics**:
| Metric | This Week | Last Week | Trend |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| [Metric] | [Value] | [Value] | [+/-] |
```

### 2. Team Announcement

```markdown
# [Announcement Title]

**TL;DR**: [One sentence summary]

---

## What's Happening
[2-3 paragraphs explaining the announcement]

## Why This Matters
[Impact and importance]

## What You Need to Do
- [ ] [Action item 1]
- [ ] [Action item 2]

## Timeline
| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| [Date] | [Event] |

## Questions?
Reach out to [contact] or ask in #[channel]
```

### 3. Leadership Update

```markdown
# [Quarter/Month] Leadership Update

**From**: [Leader Name]
**Date**: [Date]

---

## Highlights
[Top 3-5 achievements/news]

## Key Metrics
[Business metrics dashboard]

## Strategic Priorities
1. **[Priority]**: [Status and progress]
2. **[Priority]**: [Status and progress]

## Challenges & Learnings
[Honest assessment of difficulties]

## Looking Ahead
[Next period focus areas]

## Shoutouts
[Recognition for great work]
```

### 4. Project Update

```markdown
# Project Update: [Project Name]

**Status**: On Track | At Risk | Delayed
**Date**: [Date]
**PM**: [Name]

---

## Summary
[2-3 sentence project status]

## Progress Since Last Update
- [Milestone achieved]
- [Work completed]

## Current Phase
**Phase [X]**: [Name] - [Progress %]

## Key Decisions Made
- **[Decision]**: [Rationale]

## Risks & Issues
| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| [Risk] | High/Med/Low | [Action] |

## Next Steps
- [Action] - [Owner] - [Date]

## Resources Needed
- [Resource request if any]
```

### 5. Company Newsletter Section

```markdown
## [Section Title]

### [Story Headline]
[Engaging opening sentence]

[2-3 paragraphs of content]

**Key takeaway**: [One line summary]

[Optional: Image or data visualization]

---
```

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone
- Professional but approachable
- Clear and concise
- Action-oriented
- Positive but honest

### Structure
- Lead with the most important information
- Use bullet points for readability
- Include clear calls to action
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)

### Best Practices
- Know your audience
- Be specific with numbers and dates
- Acknowledge challenges honestly
- End with next steps or call to action
- Proofread before sending

## Distribution Channels

| Channel | Use For | Frequency |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Email | Formal announcements | As needed |
| Slack #general | Company-wide updates | Weekly max |
| Slack #team-* | Team updates | Daily OK |
| Confluence | Permanent records | As needed |
| All-hands | Major announcements | Monthly |

## Templates

### Quick Status (Slack)

```
:chart_with_upwards_trend: **[Team] Weekly Update**

*Progress*: Shipped X, completed Y
*Focus*: Working on Z
*Blockers*: None / [Issue needing help]

Full update: [Link]
```

### Milestone Announcement (Slack)

```
:tada: **Milestone Reached!**

[Team] just [achievement]!

Key stats:
- [Metric]: [Value]
- [Metric]: [Value]

Thanks to everyone who contributed!
```

## Formatting Tips

- Use headers to organize content
- Bold key terms and names
- Use tables for data comparison
- Include relevant links
- Add emojis sparingly in Slack (not in email)
